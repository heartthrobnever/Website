Mission Statement

Our mission is to provide a platform for student filmmakers to showcase their talents, celebrate storytelling, and inspire the next generation of creators.

Executive Board Bios

Ansh Pathapadu

President

Ansh Pathapadu is a third year Computer Science Engineering and Studio Art major with a concentration in filmmaking from Fairfax, Virginia. He formed the film festival committee because he hopes to create the opportunity for all student filmmakers to show off their work and celebrate each other. Currently, he is the Vice President of Filmmaker’s Society at UVA, and has been greatly involved in film since high school. He has won awards, such as the Oniros Film Awards for Best Short Script and Best Student Screenwriter, participated in Overcranked, and completed the Adrenaline filmmaking challenge. His favorite theme to explore is trauma. Alongside cinematography, he has played tennis all of his life and went to Nationals with the UVA Club Tennis team last year.

Tiernan Caulfield

Vice President

Tiernan Caulfield is a third year English major at the University of Virginia. He is from Ashburn, Virginia, and he attended Northern Virginia Community College for two years. There, he created NOVA filmmaking, a student organization dedicated to connecting and educating students and established the NOVA Student Film Festival, the first student-led film festival in the history of the college. He led it for two years before transferring to UVA. When he heard that students were interested in beginning a film festival, he was ecstatic to have the opportunity to apply his experience on a larger scale. His favorite film genre is drama and he has a dog named Tailz.

Gabriel Mayor-Mora

Programming Lead

Gabriel Mayor-Mora is a first year Studio Art major at the University of Virginia. Last year, he was the president of the Verde Film Festival that promotes minority-led films. He joined the Virginia Student Film Festival because of his love of films and past experiences running film festivals in high school. He enjoys making experimental films that mesh his poetry and his favorite genre is horror. Fun fact: he saw Ice Cube at the airport.

Rowan Rosenblum

Judging Lead

Rowan Rosenblum is a second year Statistics major from Fairfax, Virginia at the University of Virginia. He joined the Virginia Student Film Festival so he could help spread a love of film across the university and state college system with the hope of giving students the chance to feel unified in their passion for filmmaking. He was the president of his high school’s film club and has been a producer for 3 student films, I See You, For the Last Time, and Robbed Blind at UVA and a few bigger budget short films such as Inclusive Space. He currently works for a film production company as a script analyst and associate line producer.

Henry Pallatroni

Finance Lead

Henry Pallatroni is a fourth-year Economics Major from North Hampton, New Hampshire. He joined the film festival because he wanted to be a part of a group of motivated students looking to do something extraordinary for Virginia filmmaking. He has always loved films, and his experience working in and around the UVA film scene for the last three years has made him even more excited to help give something back in his last year. His personal favorite drama is thriller!

Kenneth Nguyen

Marketing Co-Lead

Kenneth Nguyen is a third-year student at the University of Virginia, double majoring in Film and Computer Science. With experience working on narrative and commercial projects, Kenneth has collaborated with organizations like the Black & Indigenous Feminist Features Institute, Shelley Whitman Associates, and The Moth: MainStage. His short films have been recognized at international film festivals, and he continues to develop new creative works. Kenneth is thrilled to serve as the marketing co-lead for the Virginia Student Short Film Festival.

Iresha Choudhary

Marketing Lead

Iresha Choudhary is a first year Political Philosophy and Policy major with an interest in film and law from Richmond, Virginia. She joined this festival because she really loves filmmaking and wanted to get involved as much as possible. In the past, she has had experiences directing, assistant directing, and producing films like Blinded, Two Sides of the Same Cards, and She Likes Her Coffee Black, which comments on the normalized aspects of racism in everyday life. She is honored to have had three of her screenplays produced and filmed. Last summer, she was able to attend Northwestern’s summer film program with a concentration in screenwriting. She loves writing and filming thrillers and half-serious mysteries.
